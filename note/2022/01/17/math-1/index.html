数学题1

Xiaowei Zhu / 2022-01-17

Let $x$ be an integer between $1$ and $10^{12}$, what is the probability that cubic of $x$ ends with $51$?

If $x$ ends with $51$, this means that $x^3 \equiv51\pmod{100}$.

$$\Leftrightarrow100|x^3-51$$

$$\Leftrightarrow\begin{cases}4|x^3-51 \\ 25|x^3-51\end{cases}$$

$$\Leftrightarrow\begin{cases}4|x^3+1 \\ 25|x^3-1\end{cases}$$

$$\Leftrightarrow\begin{cases}4|(x+1)(x^2-x+1) \\ 25|(x-1)(x^2+x+1)\end{cases}$$

Since $x^2-x+1$ is odd,

$$\Leftrightarrow\begin{cases}4|(x+1) \\ 25|(x-1)(x^2+x+1)\end{cases}$$

Let $x = 4k-1$, $k = 1,2,3,\cdots$. $$\Leftrightarrow25|(4k-2)(16k^2-4k+1)$$

Since $$\begin{aligned}16k^2-4k+1 &\equiv k^2-4k+1\pmod{5}\\&\equiv (k-2)^2-3\pmod{5}\end{aligned}$$

Since the square number mod 5 can only be $0,1,4$, therefore

$$5\nmid 16k^2-4k+1\Longrightarrow25\nmid 16k^2-4k+1$$

Therefore, $$2k-1 = 50m$$ Let $m = 2t-1$, then $k = 25t-12$. $$x = 100t-49$$

As a result, $$x^3\equiv51\pmod{100}\equiv x=100t-49,\quad(t=1,2,3,\cdots)$$

When $1\leq x\leq 10^{12}$, $1\leq t\leq 10^{10}$.

So, the probability is $\frac{1}{100}$.

This result can also be verified through the following code, considering $x\leq 10^3$.

k = 0
for i in range(1,10**3):
    if (i ** 3) % 100 == 51:
        print(i)
        k = k + 1
print("k={}".format(k))
